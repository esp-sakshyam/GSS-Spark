The Challenge

Why Remote Nepal Needs This

No Cellular Coverage

Mountain terrain blocks signals. Villages remain isolated with no way to call for help during emergencies.

Frequent Power Outages

Unreliable electricity means communication devices can't stay charged when they're needed most.

Delayed Rescue

Without instant communication, rescue teams learn of emergencies hours or days late—often too late.

Natural Disasters

Landslides and storms cut off physical access. The only way out is through the air—if anyone knows you need help.

3.5M+

people in Nepal's remote regions lack reliable emergency communication infrastructure

The Solution

How LifeLine Works

A mesh network of low-power devices that relay emergency signals across mountains and valleys—no cellular towers or internet required.

01

Detect

Sensors monitor health vitals, weather conditions, and manual SOS triggers from villagers.

02

Process

On-device AI analyzes data to predict emergencies before they become critical.

03

Relay

LoRa mesh network hops signals across 10+ kilometers through terrain obstacles.

04

Gateway

Central hub receives mesh data and syncs to cloud when connection is available.

05

Respond

Authorities receive real-time alerts and coordinate helicopter rescue missions.
